## 1. Adult Participant Information and Consent Template

### Study title

3C Mall Household Grocery and Meal-Planning Feasibility Pilot

### Sponsor and project lead

Ell Vii’s Automations LLC  
Project: 3C Mall  
Project Director: Lawrence Velasquez

### Why this pilot is being conducted

The purpose of this pilot is to learn whether 3C Mall’s grocery-comparison, meal-planning, and household food-budget tools are usable, technically reliable, and helpful for household planning. This is a small feasibility pilot. It is not medical treatment, nutrition therapy, financial advice, a grocery-purchasing service, or a promise of savings.

### What participation involves

Participants may be asked to:

- create or use a 3C Mall account;
- enter household-level meal and grocery preferences;
- complete planning tasks;
- answer baseline, weekly, and final questions;
- report how long planning tasks take;
- provide feedback about usefulness, confidence, and stress;
- optionally submit approved grocery receipts for comparison and reimbursement review; and
- participate in a midpoint or final interview.

Estimated time commitment: [insert expected time].

### Data that may be collected

- account email and authentication information;
- household-level preferences and planning choices;
- app activity needed to evaluate the pilot;
- survey and interview responses;
- grocery receipt information when voluntarily submitted;
- reimbursement records; and
- technical logs needed to identify errors and protect the service.

The pilot should not request an EBT card number, EBT PIN, full payment-card information, bank credentials, unnecessary medical information, or unnecessary information directly identifying minors.

### Receipt images

Receipt submission is optional unless a specific reimbursement request requires a receipt. Receipt images may contain store, date, product, price, tax, payment-type fragments, loyalty information, or other details. Participants should be instructed to cover or redact unnecessary card, account, loyalty, address, or other sensitive information before submission.

### Risks

Possible risks include:

- frustration or inconvenience;
- inaccurate or outdated estimated price information;
- differences between an estimate and the retailer’s final price;
- privacy risk associated with online information or receipt images; and
- emotional discomfort when discussing household budgeting or planning stress.

Participants should always verify final price, product availability, eligibility, substitutions, payment, and fulfillment directly with the retailer.

### Benefits

A participant may or may not personally benefit. Possible benefits include improved organization, clearer comparisons, reduced planning time, or greater confidence. These outcomes are not guaranteed.

### Voluntary participation and withdrawal

Participation is voluntary. A participant may stop at any time without penalty. Withdrawal stops future research collection. Treatment of information collected before withdrawal will follow the final consent, data-management policy, legal requirements, and grant-reporting requirements.

### Reimbursement

Eligible pilot expenses may be reimbursed only under the separate reimbursement policy. The maximum is $500 per family and the total project pool is $2,500. A valid receipt and required documentation are necessary. Participation does not guarantee full reimbursement of grocery purchases.

### Confidentiality

The project will use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards. No online system can promise absolute security. Reports should use aggregated or de-identified information whenever practical. Direct identifiers should not be published without separate permission.

## 2. Family Pilot Reimbursement Policy

### Purpose

The reimbursement pool helps offset approved grocery costs directly connected to Phase I pilot participation and receipt-supported outcome review.

### Maximum

- Up to $500 per participating family.
- No more than $2,500 across all five families.
- The cap applies to the entire project period unless the approved grant budget states otherwise.

### Required documentation

A reimbursement request must include:

- participant or family pilot code;
- legible receipt;
- retailer name;
- purchase date;
- requested amount;
- brief link to the approved pilot activity or comparison period;
- certification that the expense has not been reimbursed elsewhere; and
- preferred approved payment method.

### Eligible expenses

Only costs approved in the final pilot protocol and grant budget are eligible. Approval may be limited to grocery items or receipt periods used for the research comparison.

### Ineligible expenses

- duplicate submissions;
- altered or fabricated receipts;
- unreadable receipts that cannot be verified;
- expenses outside the approved period;
- unrelated non-grocery items unless explicitly approved;
- gift cards, cash-equivalent items, fees, or prohibited products unless expressly allowed by the grant and protocol;
- amounts previously reimbursed by another source; and
- amounts above the family or project cap.

### Review process

1. Request received and logged.
2. Receipt reviewed for eligibility and duplication.
3. Approved amount documented.
4. Payment authorized by the designated project official.
5. Payment date and reference recorded.
6. Receipt and ledger retained according to the grant and data-retention policy.

### Retailer and payment boundary

Reimbursement does not mean Ell Vii’s Automations LLC or 3C Mall sold, purchased, delivered, or processed the grocery transaction. The participant purchases directly from the retailer. The retailer remains responsible for the transaction and fulfillment.

### Disputes

Questions should be submitted to [project contact]. The Project Director will provide a written determination based on the approved policy and available documentation.

## 3. Baseline Survey

Use consistent scales at baseline and endpoint.

1. How many people are included in your household grocery planning?
2. How many grocery-shopping trips or orders do you usually complete each week?
3. Which retailers do you commonly use?
4. About how many minutes do you spend planning groceries and meals in a typical week?
5. On a 0–10 scale, how stressful is grocery and meal planning for your household?
6. On a 0–10 scale, how confident are you that your grocery decisions fit your household budget?
7. How often do planned meals get changed because of time, price, preference, or availability?
8. Which task is most difficult: meal selection, list building, unit-cost comparison, retailer comparison, household coordination, or another task?
9. What tools do you currently use?
10. What would make the pilot valuable to you?
11. What privacy or trust concern matters most?
12. Optional demographic or household-context questions may be included only when justified and approved.
