# 3C Mall Phase I Stakeholder Validation Toolkit

This toolkit supports preliminary customer discovery and letters of support. It does not convert respondents into researchers, consultants, subcontractors, or project-team members.

## Evidence handling rules

For every LinkedIn post or poll, preserve:

- exact post text;
- exact question and answer choices;
- date and time posted;
- closing date;
- intended respondent group;
- total views or impressions when available;
- total responses;
- response percentages and counts;
- screenshots;
- relevant comments;
- respondent credentials only when voluntarily public and relevant; and
- a short explanation of limitations.

Use grant language such as:

> Respondents to our preliminary LinkedIn customer-discovery campaign reported…

Do not use:

> The public believes…

## Campaign structure

Run separate posts for each audience. Mixing all audiences into one poll makes the results harder to interpret.

### 1. Families and prospective users

**Post introduction**

3C Mall is a developing household decision-support platform intended to help families organize meal planning, compare estimated grocery costs and unit prices, and keep grocery decisions in one guided place. It does not sell food, process grocery payments, or guarantee final retailer prices.

**Primary question**

Which part of grocery and meal planning creates the most difficulty for your household?

**Suggested choices**

- Comparing prices and package sizes
- Deciding what meals to make
- Staying within a grocery budget
- Coordinating preferences and schedules

**Follow-up questions**

1. Would one coordinated platform for meal planning, grocery-list organization, and estimated retailer comparison be useful to your household?
2. What information would you need to trust an estimated grocery comparison?
3. Would you prefer a household subscription, employer-sponsored access, community-sponsored access, or a free limited version?
4. What would make you stop using a tool like this?
5. Would you consider participating in a structured pilot if privacy, consent, and reimbursement terms were clearly explained?

### 2. Food scientists, nutrition professionals, and dietitians

**Post introduction**

We are seeking preliminary professional feedback on a household grocery and meal-planning decision-support concept. The platform is not intended to diagnose, treat, or replace individualized professional care.

**Primary question**

Which safeguard is most important when a consumer platform provides general meal-planning support?

**Suggested choices**

- Clear limits and disclaimers
- Transparent ingredient and nutrition sources
- Flexible support for varied eating patterns
- Professional review and update procedures

**Follow-up questions**

1. What general food-science or nutrition checks should be included before a family pilot?
2. Which claims should the platform avoid?
3. What data-source documentation would strengthen credibility?
4. What would you expect from an independent professional review?
5. Would you be open to discussing a defined paid review role after funding, subject to a written scope and agreement?

### 3. Cybersecurity, privacy, and cloud professionals

**Primary question**

For a small household food-planning pilot, which security control should be validated first?

**Suggested choices**

- Authentication and session security
- Data minimization and deletion
- Access control and audit logging
- Backup, recovery, and incident response

**Follow-up questions**

1. What information should never be collected during the pilot?
2. What controls would you expect for receipt-image storage?
3. What minimum evidence should be included in a Phase I security report?
4. What risks arise from third-party retailer and API integrations?
5. Would you consider a defined paid security-review role after funding, subject to a written agreement?

### 4. Data scientists and artificial-intelligence specialists

**Primary question**

Which measure would best demonstrate that a grocery decision-support system is producing useful results?

**Suggested choices**

- Match between estimates and receipts
- Reduced planning time
- Increased decision confidence
- Continued household use

**Follow-up questions**

1. How should a five-family feasibility pilot avoid overstating results?
2. What data-quality metrics should be tracked for product matching and unit normalization?
3. How should missing or stale price data be represented?
4. What model or automation decisions require human confirmation?
5. What would justify a larger Phase II study?

### 5. Software, systems, and quality engineers

**Primary question**

Which technical proof would matter most before expanding a household planning pilot?

**Suggested choices**

- Reliable end-to-end workflow
- Secure user-data separation
- Clear failure and recovery behavior
- Repeatable automated testing

**Follow-up questions**

1. What should be included in the technical validation matrix?
2. Which parts of the workflow should remain human-confirmed?
3. What deployment and rollback evidence should be documented?
4. What accessibility or mobile tests should be mandatory?
5. What would make the system ready for a larger pilot?

### 6. Grocery, food-access, retail, and community-program professionals

**Primary question**

Where could a household grocery decision-support platform provide the greatest value?

**Suggested choices**

- Meal planning before shopping
- Unit-cost and basket comparison
- Budget-cycle organization
- Sponsored access and education

**Follow-up questions**

1. What retailer or program boundaries must be made clear?
2. What data-source permissions or agreements may be needed?
3. What would make a sponsored-access pilot credible?
4. Which households or communities could benefit most?
5. What evidence would be needed before partnership discussions?

# Letter of Support Templates

## A. Household or prospective-user letter

**Re: Support for 3C Mall Phase I Feasibility Research**

I am writing to express support for Ell Vii’s Automations LLC’s proposed Phase I research project for 3C Mall, a household grocery-comparison, meal-planning, and food-budget decision-support platform.

From my experience as [briefly describe household or community perspective], grocery and meal planning can be difficult because [describe the real problem]. A tool that helps users organize meals, compare estimated grocery information, and review household decisions in one place could be useful if it is easy to understand, protects user information, and clearly explains that retailers control final price, availability, payment, and fulfillment.

I would be interested in [learning more / participating in a pilot / providing structured feedback] if the project is funded and the participation terms, privacy protections, and time requirements are acceptable.

This letter reflects my support and interest. It does not create a consulting, employment, or subcontracting commitment.

Sincerely,

[Name]  
[Role or household perspective]  
[Contact information]  
[Date]
